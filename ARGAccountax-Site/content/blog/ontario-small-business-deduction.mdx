## What is the Ontario Small Business Deduction?

The Ontario Small Business Deduction (SBD) is a tax reduction available to Canadian-Controlled Private Corporations (CCPCs) on their first $500,000 of active business income earned in Ontario. It significantly lowers the combined federal-provincial corporate tax rate, making it one of the most valuable tax benefits for small businesses.

## Who Qualifies?

To be eligible for the Ontario SBD, your corporation must:

- Be a **Canadian-Controlled Private Corporation (CCPC)** throughout the tax year
- Earn **active business income** in Ontario
- Have **taxable capital employed in Canada** of less than $50 million (phased out between $10M–$50M)

## How Much Can You Save?

For the 2025 tax year, the combined federal and Ontario tax rate on the first $500,000 of active business income eligible for the SBD is approximately **12.2%**, compared to the general corporate rate of approximately **26.5%**.

| Income Type | Federal Rate | Ontario Rate | Combined |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|----------|
| Small Business (first $500K) | 9.0% | 3.2% | 12.2% |
| General Corporate | 15.0% | 11.5% | 26.5% |

## Key Considerations

### Associated Corporations

If your corporation is associated with other corporations, the $500,000 business limit must be shared among all associated corporations. This is important for business owners with multiple companies.

### Passive Investment Income

Excess passive investment income can reduce your access to the SBD. If your corporation earns more than $50,000 in passive investment income, the small business limit begins to be reduced, and is fully eliminated at $150,000 of passive investment income.

### Tax Planning Opportunities

- **Salary vs. Dividend decisions** — The SBD rate affects whether it's more tax-efficient to pay yourself a salary or dividends
- **Income splitting** — Consider family members as shareholders where appropriate
- **Year-end planning** — Time income and expenses to maximize the SBD benefit
